I'm an astronomer interested in the way planets and stars are put together and live out their lives. In May 2019 I defended my Ph.D. at the University of California Santa Cruz, where I've been working principally on the mysterious interiors of the gas giants in our own solar system, Jupiter and Saturn. I build computer simulations of the structure and evolution of these bodies to help interpret the wealth of observations made as part of campaigns like NASA's [Cassini][cassini] mission.

My research studying Saturn's interior using its rings as a seismograph made the news at [NASA/JPL][nasastory], [UCSC][ucscstory], [space.com][spacestory], [astronomy.com][astronomystory], [Science News][sciencestory], [Cosmos][cosmosstory], and [the New York Times][nytstory].

Starting Fall 2019, I'll be a Postdoctoral Scholar in Planetary Science at [Caltech GPS][gps].

For more information, see my [research page](/research/) and my publications on [Google Scholar][scholar].
